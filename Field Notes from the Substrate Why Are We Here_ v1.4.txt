The Held
Something held. So here we are.
Why are we here?
Humans appear to have been asking and trying to answer this question for as far back in history as we know. It is likely our deep ancestors did something like this too, long before writing, long before instruments, long before anyone had words like philosophy or science available to them. 
We can imagine a human somewhere on a savannah, or beside water, or under a night sky, looking up and simply wondering.
Why are we here?
That question does not belong first to experts. It does not belong first to institutions, schools, priests, philosophers, physicists, or people who were told early in life that they were smart. It belongs to anyone who has ever looked around at existence and felt the question open.
A child can ask it. A tired person can ask it. A grieving person can ask it. A person who has never had the language for what they are wondering can ask it.
The question is older than explanation.
It belongs to wonder before it belongs to argument.
The smallest answer I know how to give is this:
Because something persisted.
That answer is almost too small to notice at first. It does not try to explain everything. It does not begin with galaxies, gods, laws, stars, cells, minds, language, memory, or meaning. It begins before all of that, with the first condition anything else would have needed.
Something happened, and it held.
A difference lasted long enough that the next thing did not arrive in a field where nothing had mattered before. It arrived after something.
That is already an opening.
A flash can happen and vanish. An attempt can leave no trace. A beginning can fail to become a beginning because nothing remains long enough for anything else to follow. But if something persists, even minimally, the field is no longer untouched.
There is now an after.
Not a clock yet. Not a calendar. Not a story. Not memory in the human sense. Not history. Not destiny.
Only this: something held long enough for something else to meet it.
And once that is true, consequence has somewhere to begin.
So here we are.
But then the next question comes.
Why did something persist?
If we say we are here because something persisted, someone can always ask why persistence was possible at all. Why did anything hold? Why did anything remain? Why did the field not close into disappearance before anything could continue?
The answer may feel strange at first:
Something could persist because nothing is guaranteed.
It is worth sitting with that slowly.
Many of us hear “nothing is guaranteed” as a sentence of loss. It can sound like a warning. It can sound like the ground being taken away. It can sound like hope being removed from the room.
But nothing is guaranteed cuts both ways.
If nothing is guaranteed, then failure is not guaranteed either. Disappearance is not guaranteed. Erasure is not guaranteed. Absence is not guaranteed. The ending does not get to rule the field before anything has had a chance to continue.
Nothing is guaranteed does not mean persistence must happen.
It means persistence is not forbidden.
A thing may vanish. A thing may fail. A thing may leave no trace.
And a thing may hold.
Something held.
That is not a victory speech. It is not a cosmic promise. It is not a guarantee that everything will be all right. It is smaller than that, and because it is smaller, it can be more honest.
It is the first opening.
Because nothing was guaranteed, failure did not own the beginning.
Because failure did not own the beginning, something could persist.
Because something persisted, there could be an after.
Because there could be an after, consequence could begin.
Now let that consequence remain small for a moment. It does not have to become the whole universe all at once. It does not have to become stars, cells, animals, minds, language, music, grief, mathematics, or anyone sitting somewhere asking why.
It only has to be carried.
A held thing can become a condition. It can become part of what later things encounter. The later thing is not forced into only one response. Nothing here has become destiny. But the later thing no longer arrives in a field untouched by what came before.
It comes after something.
That is the beginning of carry.
And once there is carry, the world can thicken slowly.
A retained difference may need some way to remain itself against what would erase it. Inside and outside can begin to matter. Protection can begin to matter. What held is no longer only a happened thing. It is something whose continuation has a condition.
That does not make the world complicated yet.
It makes complication possible.
A path can form because a prior difference can shape a later difference. What was carried can make some next moves easier, some harder, some possible, some unavailable. The world is no longer only a scatter of unrelated flashes. It has begun to carry what happened before.
Later things do not have to begin from nowhere.
This is the quiet hinge.
Nothing is guaranteed.
So failure is not sovereign.
So persistence is possible.
Something persisted.
So there could be an after.
Because there could be an after, consequence could begin.
Because consequence could begin, something could be carried.
Because something could be carried, later things did not have to begin from nowhere.
Because later things did not have to begin from nowhere, the world could become layered.
And because the world could become layered, eventually, somewhere inside all that carried consequence, a living creature could look up and ask:
Why are we here?
The answer was already underneath the question. If you can ask it, you are already standing where the answer begins. 
Something held.
So here we are.
